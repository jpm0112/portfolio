About Me

I'm a fifth-year PhD candidate in Industrial and Systems Engineering at Auburn University. My research covers operations research, machine learning, optimization, and simulation, with a dissertation on advanced methods for complex logistics problems. I've been involved in the INFORMS student chapter since 2022, serving as Secretary, Vice-President, and President.

My research has spanned two-dimensional truck loading optimization, military aircraft and crew scheduling for U.S. Air Force wargaming simulations, deep learning applied to illicit supply chain detection, freight rate forecasting with explainable AI, and drone-based order-picking in warehouses. In 2024, I spent a semester as a visiting researcher at NTNU in Norway, developing closed-loop supply chain models.

I'm originally from Chile and fluent in both Spanish and English. I started in research doing humanitarian logistics simulation at Universidad de los Andes, and I'm now looking for industry roles where I can apply optimization, data science, and machine learning to real-world problems.

Freight Rate Forecasting with XAI

Applied Temporal Fusion Transformers, LSTMs, and attention models to predict shipping freight rates in Chilean ports. Used LIME and SHAP for interpretability and Bayesian methods for hyperparameter tuning. The explainability analysis revealed key drivers behind rate fluctuations, providing actionable insights for maritime logistics decision-making.

Multi-Objective Flight Scheduling

Built optimization models and heuristics for U.S. Air Force wargaming simulations, scheduling military aircraft and crew across missions while balancing range, refueling, and personnel constraints. Developed MILP formulations and metaheuristic solvers including Simulated Annealing, Tabu Search, and Genetic Algorithms.

Closed-Loop Supply Chain Optimization

Built a stochastic programming framework to integrate product development and design decisions into supply chains facing disruptive events, informed by two Norwegian circular manufacturing case studies. Results showed resilience improvements of 4–5% and budget savings of 2–10% while enhancing sustainability and circularity. Published in Omega (2025), done during my visit to NTNU, Norway.

Stochastic ProgrammingMILPSupply Chain

04

2D Truck Loading Optimization

Developed a three-stage matheuristic combining MILP models to solve a 2D vehicle loading problem for a Colombian motorcycle assembler with a heterogeneous fleet. The approach maximizes on-time deliveries, minimizes trucks used, and reduces rehandles during unloading. Results showed increased demand fulfillment over 1D methods, especially under relaxed credit constraints.

MILPMatheuristic2D Bin PackingLogistics
05

Drone Order-Picking Optimization

Developed cooperative drone-worker order-picking models for warehouses, progressing from independent routing to fully coordinated systems with synchronization, battery constraints, and multi-trip drone scheduling. Built MILP formulations using CVRP with MTZ subtour elimination and Big-M linearizations, minimizing makespan across multiple scenarios.
